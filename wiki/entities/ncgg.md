NCGG is Japan's national research and medical center dedicated to geriatrics and gerontology. NUCU teams visit it during the Nagoya phase of the program, including a tour of its **Assistive Robot Center**, which develops and helps commercialize assistive technologies and care robots for older adults. The center's stated mission (per the Program Guide) is to connect technological "seeds" with real elder-care needs, provide testing fields for robot development from a longevity-medicine perspective, advance rehabilitation-engineering research, and inform policy.

The visit is framed as a deliberate contrast to the program's startup-ecosystem site visits ([[station-ai]], [[tongali]]): where those show business models and market opportunity, NCGG shows how clinical evidence, user-centered design, safety evaluation, and long-term-care needs shape technology development from the research-institution side. Students are encouraged to see robotics not as advanced technology for its own sake but as a tool for dignity, independence, safety, mobility, and quality of life — a framing meant to sharpen how they design their own NUCU startup concepts around [[reiwa-era-shrinking-society]] challenges.

NCGG sits within the same site-visit day as **Professor Hirofumi Aoki**'s driving-simulation and mobility research at NIC (Nagoya University's National Innovation Complex) — Aoki's work bridges engineering, medicine, and public policy to pilot next-generation mobility (e.g., autonomous shuttle trials) for both urban and rural Japanese communities facing transportation barriers as their populations age. See [[japan-aging-society-healthcare]] for the broader healthcare-system context these visits sit inside.
