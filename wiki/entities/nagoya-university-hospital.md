Nagoya University Hospital, on the Tsurumai Campus, is where NUCU's orientation, hospital tour, and patient/caregiver market-research surveys take place. Its **xR Center** introduces students to extended reality (xR — a collective term for VR, AR, and MR) used in medical education, clinical training, and healthcare innovation: visualizing anatomy, simulating clinical scenarios, and supporting procedural training so students and professionals can practice without patient risk. The Program Guide frames the xR Center visit as showing how innovation can emerge from *within* a university hospital — pairing academic medicine, clinical practice, engineering, and education — as a contrast to the market-facing innovation shown at [[station-ai]].

During the Nagoya field days, NUCU teams conduct **surveys and interviews** with patients, families, and caregivers in the hospital waiting areas as primary market research for their startup concepts — a hands-on complement to the classroom lean-canvas and prompt-engineering training covered in [[prompt-engineering]].

Session 7's guest lecture (delivered by Hasegawa-sensei, a Nagoya University physician, with Tanaka-sensei of [[tongali]] also joining) used the hospital and its home-care network as the entry point into a broader discussion of [[japan-aging-society-healthcare]] — Japan's universal coverage system, age-based cost sharing, the Long-Term Care Insurance program, and the case study of Dr. Ryoichi Ichihashi's home-hospice practice, which emphasizes quality of life and patient-directed care over pure longevity. That lecture is the primary source for the healthcare-system concept page; this entity page covers the physical site and the xR Center specifically.
