Tongali is Nagoya University's entrepreneurship education and startup-support initiative, launched in 2016 to foster entrepreneurship across Central Japan's academic communities (a consortium the program describes as spanning 28 universities in the Nagoya/Aichi/Gifu/central-Japan region). It has helped numerous university-led startups grow through funding and incubation support, and provides tuition-free entrepreneurial education, co-working space, makers' studios (3D printers and other prototyping tools), and a global network of mentors and investors. Participation is free for anyone who wants to learn about entrepreneurship, not just Nagoya University students.

Tongali is a recurring touchpoint for [[nucu-program]]: NUCU teams get a Tongali/Nagoya University entrepreneurship overview during the [[station-ai]] site visit, and Tongali's research administrator, **Sawako Tanaka** ("Tanaka-sensei"), is the Nagoya-side coordinator for NUCU site visits — she has joined CU Boulder class sessions by video call (session 7, session 12) to help debrief the design sprint and plan the Japan itinerary, and previously organized entrepreneurial workshops on urban renewal that paired international students with local Nagoya residents to identify community problems.

Tongali functions as the education/talent-pipeline half of Nagoya's startup ecosystem, complementing the physical hub-and-capital role played by [[station-ai]]. Both are referenced in Session 6 as part of the broader government-backed [[japan-startup-ecosystem]] push (the 2022 ¥10 trillion investment, startup visas for foreign founders, and the "Society 5.0" vision).
