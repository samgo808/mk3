Nakatsugawa City (southeastern Gifu Prefecture) is a rural, depopulated town about an hour from Nagoya that NUCU teams visit as a field trip to observe healthcare and demographic challenges outside the big cities — the rural counterpart to the urban site visits at [[nagoya-university-hospital]], [[station-ai]], and [[ncgg]]. It sits on the historic Nakasendo route between Edo (Tokyo) and Kyoto and is known for kuri kinton confectionery and the nearby **Iwamura Castle Town**, a preserved Edo-period castle town associated with Lady Otsuya, a rare female castle ruler in Japanese history.

The clinical centerpiece of the visit is **Agi Clinic**, a community medical facility run under the Nakatsugawa City National Health Insurance system, led by **Dr. Nobutaro Ban**. Dr. Ban is a Nagoya University School of Medicine graduate who trains Nagoya medical students in community-based and rural medicine, has international experience (including study in the US), and focuses on geriatric medicine, home healthcare, chronic disease management, and palliative care for a rapidly aging, resource-limited rural population. NUCU students attend a lecture and facility tour led by Dr. Ban, then visit an elderly care house (Cyclamen) for a tour and interviews.

The Program Guide frames this visit as giving students direct, physical exposure to the labor shortages, cultural erosion, and social-instability risks tied to Japan's demographic decline ([[reiwa-era-shrinking-society]]) — intended to sharpen, not soften, the [[japan-aging-society-healthcare]] discussion by grounding it outside a major hospital system.
