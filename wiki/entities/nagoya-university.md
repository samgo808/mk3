Nagoya University is the Japanese partner institution for [[nucu-program]], hosting CU Boulder students for the Nagoya phase of the May program and pairing them with Nagoya University student teams for the design sprint and final pitch. Founded in 1871 as a provisional hospital and medical school for the Nagoya Domain, it is one of Japan's premier research universities and home to multiple Nobel laureates.

**Two main campuses relevant to the program**:
- **Tsurumai Campus** — School of Medicine and University Hospital. NUCU orientation, hospital tours, the xR Center visit, and faculty meeting rooms are here. See [[nagoya-university-hospital]].
- **Higashiyama Campus** — includes the Toyoda Auditorium (a 1960 Brutalist landmark, National Tangible Cultural Property since 2011) and the NIC (National Innovation Complex), whose 1F co-creation space "Idea Stoa" hosts team project work and the final presentations.

**Education initiatives** mentioned in the Program Guide: NOVI+A (community-oriented medical education), CIBoG (informatics + biomedical sciences), and AI-MAILs (academia-industry bridge for medical AI leaders). Nagoya University hosted the 6th annual GAME conference (a consortium of world-class medical schools) in November 2022.

**Key people**: Masahisa Katsuno (Dean, School of Medicine), Hideki Kasuya (Vice Dean for International Affairs), Sawako Tanaka (Research Administrator, coordinates the Tongali entrepreneurship program and NUCU site-visit logistics — see [[tongali]]), Hitoki Hasegawa (Jr. Associate Professor, on-the-ground emergency contact for the program).

Nagoya University's entrepreneurship arm, [[tongali]], and its adjacent innovation hub, [[station-ai]] (located near Tsurumai Campus), are the program's primary startup-ecosystem site visits. [[ncgg]] (National Center for Geriatrics and Gerontology) and [[prevent-inc]] round out the healthcare-innovation site visits.

Students stay at **Yamate South International Residence**, a Nagoya University dormitory near Higashiyama Campus, during the Nagoya portion of the trip (see [[nucu-2026-itinerary]] for logistics).
