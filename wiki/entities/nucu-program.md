NUCU ("Build in Japan": AI & Automation for Tomorrow's Societies) is a joint program between the University of Colorado Boulder and Nagoya University. It runs as a 3-credit CU Boulder course, **BAIM 3300**, taught primarily by Matt Brady (Professor, Leeds School of Business) with Samuel Goodman as co-instructor leading Japan-culture content and the AI teaching-assistant project ([[makoto-kun]]). Students also earn micro-credentials through CU's Innovation & Entrepreneurship office and Ludus Labs.

The course challenges international student teams (CU Boulder + Nagoya University) to design tech-enabled solutions that help societies **adapt to demographic change** — not to "solve" population decline outright. The throughline is Japan as a "living lab" for aging-society and shrinking-society challenges that other countries, including the US, are expected to face soon. See [[reiwa-era-shrinking-society]] for the demographic core of that thesis.

**Structure**: a spring-semester virtual course (weekly evening lectures, roughly Jan–April) culminating in a 15-day in-person program in Japan (Tokyo and Nagoya) in May, where CU teams pair with Nagoya University student teams to pitch startup ideas at a final presentation event. See [[nucu-2026-itinerary]] for the day-by-day 2026 schedule and [[nucu-program-policies-safety]] for conduct, health, and safety rules.

**Curriculum pillars**, drawn from the semester's lecture sequence:
- Historical/cultural context: [[meiji-restoration]], [[postwar-economic-miracle]], [[lost-decades]], [[reiwa-era-shrinking-society]]
- Technical literacy: [[osi-tcpip-networking]], [[large-language-models]], [[ai-model-taxonomy]], [[retrieval-augmented-generation]], [[prompt-engineering]], [[vibe-coding-nocode-tools]]
- Applied methodology: [[design-thinking-sprint]] (the virtual design sprint with Nagoya University teams), field research and site visits during the Japan trip
- Healthcare/aging focus: [[japan-aging-society-healthcare]], guest sessions with Nagoya University faculty (Hasegawa-sensei, Tanaka-sensei)

**Key people mentioned across lectures**: Matt Brady and Samuel "Sam" Goodman (CU instructors), Hitoki Hasegawa (Nagoya University, Jr. Associate Professor, emergency contact), Sawako Tanaka (Nagoya University, Tongali entrepreneurship program, coordinates site visits), Sarah Westmoreland (CU Education Abroad), Masahisa Katsuno (Dean, Nagoya University School of Medicine), Hideki Kasuya (Vice Dean for International Affairs).

**Nagoya University partnership**: see [[nagoya-university]] for the host institution and its entrepreneurship initiative [[tongali]]. Key Japan-side site visits include [[station-ai]], [[ncgg]], [[nagoya-university-hospital]], [[prevent-inc]], and [[nakatsugawa-agi-clinic]].

The program's marketing framing (from the recruitment brochure) emphasizes Japan as "a living lab of the future" for population collapse, cross-cultural collaboration via a design sprint, expert-led workshops on AI & Ethics, and career-launching international experience. Student testimonials describe the value of open-ended, no-guidelines problem solving and cross-cultural teamwork as the program's biggest takeaways.
