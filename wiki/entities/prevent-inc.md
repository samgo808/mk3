PREVENT Inc. is a Nagoya-based health-tech startup focused on preventing lifestyle-related diseases (diabetes, heart disease, stroke) and reducing hospital readmissions, particularly among elderly patients, by shifting healthcare's focus from treatment to prevention. Its core service is data-driven health coaching and remote support: personalized post-discharge care plans, regular check-ins by phone or app, and behavior-modification strategies, delivered in partnership with hospitals, local governments, and insurance providers.

PREVENT is presented to NUCU students (via a session led by Mr. Hagiwara at Nagoya University's Tsurumai Campus) as a worked example of the kind of startup the program wants teams to design: a for-profit company addressing a concrete consequence of Japan's aging population ([[reiwa-era-shrinking-society]], [[japan-aging-society-healthcare]]) by combining medical research, behavioral science, and digital technology to extend care beyond the hospital. Its stated ambition is to help Japan — and potentially other aging societies — reduce healthcare costs and improve long-term outcomes, directly mirroring the NUCU program challenge of designing tech-enabled solutions that help societies adapt to demographic change.
