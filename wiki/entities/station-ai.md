STATION Ai is described in the Program Guide as Japan's largest innovation terminal, located in Aichi Prefecture near Nagoya University's Tsurumai Campus. More than 700 startups, partner companies, VCs, and universities (Japanese and international) participate in it to create new businesses. NUCU teams visit it during the Nagoya phase of the program (a briefing from the Aichi Prefecture government, an overview of [[tongali]], and a facility tour).

Beyond event space and offices, the facility includes a fitness gym and tech lab for members, plus a café/restaurant, hotel, event space, and the public-facing "Aichi Founders' Hall." Its stated mission is to integrate established industries with emerging businesses across Aichi and the Tokai region — one of the most industry-dense parts of Japan. STATION Ai's own description of its work breaks into three areas: startup support (business creation, program/event management), facility maintenance and management, and fund management (investing in STATION Ai members).

STATION Ai is one of the concrete examples behind the [[japan-startup-ecosystem]] push described in Session 6 — the Japanese government's ~$67 billion (10 trillion yen) 2022 investment aimed at creating 100 unicorns and 100,000 new startups by 2026 (only seven unicorns had been produced as of that lecture). STATION Ai represents the physical infrastructure side of that strategy: shared space, capital, and cross-pollination between corporates and startups in one hub, mirroring how Nagoya University's [[tongali]] program supplies the talent and education pipeline.

For students, STATION Ai is presented as a business-model-and-market-opportunity counterpoint to the clinical/research approach shown at [[ncgg]] and [[nagoya-university-hospital]] — the Program Guide explicitly frames site visits as contrasting "business models, digital platforms, and market opportunities" (startups) against "clinical evidence, user-centered design, safety evaluation" (research institutions).
