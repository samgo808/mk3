The 2026 in-person portion of [[nucu-program]] runs **May 14–28 (15 days / 14 nights)**, covering Tokyo and Nagoya, for roughly 20 CU Boulder students, one CU chaperone, and one CoHo staff member. See [[nucu-program-policies-safety]] for conduct/health rules and [[design-thinking-sprint]] for the sprint methodology behind the team projects presented at the end of the trip.

**Phase 1 — Arrival & Tokyo startup ecosystem (May 14–17, CU only)**: arrival at Narita; visits to Google for Startups and Techstars Tokyo (startup-ecosystem exposure); a culture day at Sanja Matsuri in Asakusa; travel to Nagoya via Shinkansen.

**Phase 2 — Nagoya fieldwork and site visits (May 18–22/23, CU & Nagoya University students together)**: this is the core of the program, held while NU students are invited to join "shaded" (marked CU & NU) activities. Sequence: orientation and a hospital/xR Center tour at [[nagoya-university-hospital]]; an Aichi Prefecture briefing plus tours of [[tongali]] and [[station-ai]], including sessions with Prof. Masakatsu Fukai (medical innovation/industry-academia collaboration) and Dr. Shintaro Oyama (clinical-needs-driven innovation), and a session from [[prevent-inc]] (Mr. Hagiwara); a mobility/robotics day at [[ncgg]] (Assistive Robot Center tour) and a driving-simulation session with Prof. Hirofumi Aoki; a rural field trip to [[nakatsugawa-agi-clinic]] (Dr. Ban's lecture, a tour of Iwamura Castle Town, and an elderly-care-house visit); a market-research/survey day at Nagoya University Hospital; and final team presentations at NIC's "Idea Stoa," followed by a sushi party. Students stay at Yamate South International Residence (Nagoya University dormitory, single rooms with private bath/kitchenette).

**Phase 3 — Final presentations & Tokyo return (May 23–28, CU only after May 23)**: an optional culture day (a Chunichi Dragons baseball game was floated for 2026); Shinkansen return to Tokyo; a National Diet of Japan visit; a CU Buffs alumni/mentor dinner reception at the Tokyo American Club; a free/self-reflection day; departure.

**How this connects to the design sprint**: the session 12 lecture reviews a video from the prior year's trip covering the same categories of site visit (medical xR tech, hospital surveys, Station Ai, autonomous-vehicle research, a rural autonomous shuttle pilot, a renovated-school community space, a senior care home, and a formal town-hall pitch to a mayor and local officials) as a preview for 2026 students, and explicitly asks students to help prioritize which industries (healthcare, robotics, AI, drones, etc.) they most want to see via a site-visit survey — Tanaka-sensei of [[tongali]] is the Nagoya-side point person for arranging these based on student input.

**Logistics notes specific to travel**: intercity travel uses the Shinkansen (Tokyo↔Nagoya); local Nagoya transit is primarily subway/bus/train, with charter buses for the rural field trip; bus fare in Nagoya is a flat ¥210 for adults. See [[nucu-program-policies-safety]] for insurance, packing, and emergency-contact details that apply throughout this itinerary.
