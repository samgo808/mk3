Raw transcript of NUCU session 13 (April 14, 2026), taught by Matt Brady, combining a guest briefing from Sarah Westmoreland (CU Education Abroad) on trip logistics/health insurance/safety, program housekeeping (team voting deadlines, the site-visit survey, an MSBA graduate-program plug, curated job-board postings), and a hands-on no-code/vibe-coding lesson (Glide, then Lovable).

**Key claims covered**: post-acceptance study-abroad requirements (health and wellness worksheet, passport checks, flight booking confirmation); the international health-insurance reimbursement model, mental-health coverage, and the emergency-contact chain (Sam/Matt/Tisha first, then CU's office, not parents first); Japan's travel-advisory level (1) and the Adderall/restricted-medication compliance note; team-voting and site-visit-survey deadlines and the instruction to re-engage disengaged Nagoya University teammates; a live Glide demo (spreadsheet → app, Pexels image API, Gemini "smart column" callouts) followed by a "vibe coding" explanation and live Lovable demo (a Studio-Ghibli-styled workout/sleep tracker PWA built from a single natural-language prompt); a broad-to-narrow prompting methodology for app-building; student-led demos including a Claude Code/VS Code Japan-demographics simulation map.

**Why it was added**: the study-abroad logistics half is the primary source for trip health/safety policy; the vibe-coding half is the primary source for the no-code/low-code tool landscape taught alongside Claude Code (session 12).
