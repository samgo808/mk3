Raw marketing/recruitment document for [[nucu-program]] ("NUCU 'Build in Japan' AI & Automation for Tomorrow's Societies"), covering the program's pitch to prospective CU Boulder students: the CU Boulder × Nagoya University partnership, the BAIM 3300 course structure and dual micro-credentials (CU Innovation & Entrepreneurship, Ludus Labs), the core societal-challenge framing (aging populations, labor shortages, urban-rural disparity, mental health/social stability), the 15-day Japan itinerary at a high level, and a "Top 5 Reasons to Join NUCU" pitch plus a substantial block of prior-cohort student testimonials and advice to future participants.

**Key claims**: positions Japan as "a living lab of the future" for population collapse; emphasizes design thinking, lean canvas planning, and primary market research as the program's methodological core; culminates in a final pitch competition in Japan judged by business and healthcare entrepreneurship experts. Testimonials consistently cite open-ended (no fixed guidelines) problem-solving, cross-cultural teamwork, and field research skills as the most valued takeaways, alongside surprise at how transferable the shrinking-society problem is to students' home countries (Colombia, unspecified others are referenced by testimonial authors).

**Why it was added**: the primary source for the program's own public framing/pitch, distinct from the operational NUCU2026_Program_Guide_RAG.txt document (which covers the detailed day-by-day logistics). Updated → [[nucu-program]].
