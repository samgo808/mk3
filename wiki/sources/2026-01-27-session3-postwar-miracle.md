Raw transcript of NUCU session 3 (January 27, 2026), "Reinvention After Collapse: Economic Reconstruction Japan from Shōwa to Heisei (Post-war)," the second of Samuel Goodman's three Japan-history lectures, with course-logistics housekeeping from Matt Brady at the start (quiz schedule, the discussion-board article-sharing assignment, a preview of upcoming Figma/prompt-engineering content, mention of recursive language models and "recursive metacognition" as a forward teaser) and a guest Q&A with Leeds alum Alex Mitchell noted at the end (not captured in this transcript's cut).

**Key claims covered**: Japan's WWII-era territorial peak and 1945 devastation; MITI's role in selecting and financing strategic industries; the FILP citizen-savings financing mechanism; lifetime employment ("salaryman") culture; Akio Morita/Sony and the Bulova brand-licensing story; the Walkman's success; the real-estate/asset bubble and its 1990s collapse (interest-rate hikes, ~$10T wealth wiped out, "zombie companies"); the Lost Decades and Galapagos syndrome (i-mode, Sharp, Toshiba); monozukuri craftsmanship culture and Japan's large domestic market as reasons innovation stayed inward-facing; the "shrink gracefully" / kintsugi reframe.

Also includes a live Q&A on Japan's WWII-era atrocities and historical memory (instructor recommends the podcast *Supernova in the East* by Dan Carlin), and a substantial second half demonstrating early **Makoto-kun** builds: a "Charlie Munger investment assistant" custom GPT, Makoto-kun 1.0 (built in ChatGPT/GPTs with a knowledge base of program brochures), and a personal "Weela AI" experiment (a StoryWorth-based conversational recreation of the instructor's mother, framed as a technology response to loneliness in aging societies).
