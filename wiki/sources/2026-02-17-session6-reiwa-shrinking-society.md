Raw transcript of NUCU session 6 (February 17, 2026), "Reinvention Without a Reset Button: Societal Redesign, Japan in the Reiwa Era" — the third of Samuel Goodman's Japan-history lectures, combined with a live Makoto-kun 2.0 tech demo at the end. Includes classroom breakout-session dialogue (ice-cream-flavor grouping icebreaker) largely intact.

**Key claims covered**: the Reiwa imperial-era naming convention; Japan's fertility-rate collapse (3.3 → ~1.2), inverting population pyramid, and the "2025 problem"; marriage decline and its direct tie to birth rates (only ~2% of Japanese births are outside marriage); karoshi (death by overwork), citing the NHK journalist Miwa Sato case; ohitorisama solo culture (solo dining, solo weddings, holographic-partner marriage); silver-economy product shifts (Unicharm adult diapers, Itoen thickened tea); kodokushi (solitary death) and a Tokyo Harbor ash-scattering funeral service; rural school/municipality closures and government countermeasures (relocation incentives, a Tokyo dating app); immigration policy limits (~3–5% population, government targeting 10%, but research showing immigration only slows rather than reverses decline); the kintsugi "shrink gracefully" reframe; Japan's startup-ecosystem push (¥10T investment, 100-unicorn target reaching only 7, startup visas, Society 5.0).

The lecture then transitions into a live **RAG** (retrieval-augmented generation) and Makoto-kun 2.0/n8n architecture explanation and demo — the first appearance of vector-database/embedding concepts in the curriculum, expanded on in session 12.
