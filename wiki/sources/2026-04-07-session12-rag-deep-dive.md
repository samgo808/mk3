Raw transcript of NUCU session 12 (April 7, 2026), taught solo by Samuel Goodman (Matt Brady traveling), covering four distinct segments: a hands-on RAG/Makoto-kun 2.3 architecture build-along; a Claude Code live demo; a design-sprint debrief with Sawako Tanaka (Tongali) joining remotely; and a Japanese-survival-phrases mini-lesson.

**Key claims covered**: the full RAG ingestion pipeline (transcript cleaning → GitHub upload → n8n chunking/embedding → vector database, reaching 347 total chunks) and retrieval flow (Discord → webhook → n8n agent → vector similarity search → LLM generation), including a live retrieval failure and successful retry, and an explanation of embeddings/dot-product similarity; a Claude Code demo editing and redeploying a live marketing website (GitHub → Vercel), building a small local "senior tech support" business site and a teen driving-hours-tracker app, and discussion of a (later scandal-tainted) claim about a two-person, AI-tooled $1.8B healthcare company; a design-sprint debrief reiterating that teams should design *around* demographic-change challenges rather than "solving" population decline, a recap video of the prior year's Japan site visits, and a real-time discussion of how to get consensus votes from Nagoya University teammates who had already returned to in-person classes; a plug for David Deutsch's *The Beginning of Infinity* as the instructor's epistemology touchstone; and the Japanese survival-phrase mini-lesson (itadakimasu, gochisōsama, hajimemashite, yoroshiku onegaishimasu, dōzo, dōmo, ganbatte).
