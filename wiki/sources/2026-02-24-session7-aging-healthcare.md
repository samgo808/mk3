Raw transcript of NUCU session 7 (February 24, 2026), a guest lecture delivered remotely by Hasegawa-sensei (Nagoya University), opening with an NHK documentary clip about Dr. Ryoichi Ichihashi's home-hospice practice, followed by a lecture on Japan's healthcare and long-term-care systems, then live Q&A with both Hasegawa-sensei and Sawako Tanaka (Tongali) joining by video, moderated in-room by Matt Brady.

**Key claims covered**: the documentary's "good death" framing and the statistic that ~30% of Japanese people no longer view longevity itself positively; universal health coverage since 1961; age-based cost-sharing percentages (roughly 30% for adults, 20% for ages 70–84, 10% for 75+ under a dedicated 2008 insurance program); monthly out-of-pocket cost caps; a Japan-vs-US pricing comparison (nationally standardized vs. fragmented negotiated pricing); the 2000 Long-Term Care Insurance program and the structural reasons Japan defaults to home-based elder care (patient preference, cost, insufficient facility beds); doctors' freedom to open practices anywhere, and its access-vs-distribution tradeoffs; rural-vs-urban transportation and elder-mobility disparities, including Japan having no upper driving-age limit and the resulting accident/policy tension; the thin geriatrics-specialty pipeline and government scholarship incentives to address it; workforce-shrinkage effects on rural vs. urban regions specifically.

**Why it was added**: the program's dedicated healthcare-system lecture, directly informing the aging-related startup challenges NUCU teams are asked to design around, and grounding the site visits to [[nagoya-university-hospital]], [[ncgg]], and [[nakatsugawa-agi-clinic]].
