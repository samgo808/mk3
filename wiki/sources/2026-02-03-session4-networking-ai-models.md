Raw transcript of NUCU session 4 (February 3, 2026), taught solo by Matt Brady as a "technology primer" covering networking fundamentals and AI model taxonomy. Opens with course logistics (quiz 1 averaged 91%, ungraded-on-a-curve philosophy, quiz cadence going forward).

**Key claims covered**: the seven-layer OSI model and four-layer TCP/IP model, explained via troubleshooting analogies; a tangential aside on trades (electricians, plumbers) becoming lucrative careers near AI data center construction, and the Indianapolis Google data-center community-pushback anecdote (energy/water/noise concerns); the LLM/SLM/LCM(latent-consistency)/LCM(large-concept)/RLM model taxonomy, illustrated with the "boombox → headphones → watch speaker" miniaturization analogy; a classroom discussion of student experiences with ChatGPT, Gemini, Copilot, Claude, and Grok, including enterprise/compliance constraints on locked-down corporate Copilot deployments; DeepSeek and "Claudebot" as examples of smaller/local/open-source model deployment; a "recursive language models" teaser tied to context-window degradation; the 5P prompt-engineering framework with a live Zapier/Salesforce/FireCrawl/Gemini lead-enrichment demo.

**Why it was added**: establishes the technical vocabulary (networking layers, model types, prompting) that sessions 5, 6, 12, and 13 build directly on. Updated → [[osi-tcpip-networking]], [[ai-model-taxonomy]], [[prompt-engineering]].
