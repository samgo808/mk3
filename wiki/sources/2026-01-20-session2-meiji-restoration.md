Raw transcript of NUCU session 2 (January 20, 2026), the first of Samuel Goodman's three-part Japan history lecture series, subtitled "Reinvention Under Pressure: Institutional Transformation Japan from Edo to Meiji (Pre-war)." Delivered live in the BAIM 3300 classroom (Leeds School of Business, CU Boulder) with audio/video capture — the transcript includes classroom breakout-session dialogue between students, left largely intact in the raw file (unlike later cleaned versions fed into [[makoto-kun]]'s knowledge base).

**Key claims covered**: Edo-period feudal hierarchy; first Portuguese contact (1543) and gun technology transfer; Christianity's spread and 1614 suppression; the 1637 Shimabara uprising; ~200 years of Sakoku isolation (1639–1853) and its cultural legacy (ethnic homogeneity, high-context communication style); Commodore Perry's 1853 "black ships" and the Convention of Kanagawa; the Meiji-era centralization of currency, taxation, education, and industry; the phase-out of the samurai class and the 1877 Satsuma Rebellion; Japan's wins over China (1894) and Russia (1905) and entry into the League of Nations.

**Why it was added**: establishes the historical/cultural foundation for the course's central thesis (Japan as a repeat "reinventor" of itself under external pressure), which recurs through [[postwar-economic-miracle]], [[lost-decades]], and [[reiwa-era-shrinking-society]].
