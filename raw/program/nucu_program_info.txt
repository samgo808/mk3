## Program Overview

"NUCU 'Build in Japan' AI & Automation for Tomorrow's Societies" is an initiative between Nagoya University (NU) and the University of Colorado、Boulder (CU). It empowers students to innovate, collaborate, and make a real impact on the global stage. The NUCU Program is a 3-credit academic course at the University of Colorado, Boulder called BAIM 3300. The course also offers two micro-credentials. One micro-credential through the CU Innovation & Entrepreneurship and one micro-credential through Ludus Labs.

This experiential learning program challenges students to build tech-driven startups that address the complex impacts of a shrinking global population. You will work as international teams within the cohort to design and launch ventures that leverage AI, automation, advanced mobility, and other next-generation technologies.

Through a hands-on curriculum using lean canvas planning, design thinking, and primary market research, you will develop a complete business proposal from concept to execution. The program culminates in a final pitch competition in Japan, where you will present your innovative solution to a panel of experts in business and healthcare entrepreneurship.

The course focuses on creating viable, technology-driven solutions for critical challenges facing eastern and western societies, including:

    Aging populations and the development of advanced elder care technology
    Labor shortages and opportunities for workforce automation
    Urban-rural disparities and smart infrastructure adaptation
    Mental health and new digital tools for enhancing social stability

This interdisciplinary course prepares students for leadership in AI-driven global entrepreneurship and provides real-world startup experience in an international setting via a global intensive program.

## Why Japan?

Japan serves as an ideal laboratory for this course due to its unique demographic
challenges, including an aging population and a societal commitment to
technological innovation. These factors make Japan a dynamic testing ground for AI
& Robotics aimed at improving quality of life and health. The country's advanced
infrastructure and openness to digital transformation provide a real-world context in
which students can develop, test, and reﬁne their AI-driven innovations.

## Top 5 Reasons to Join NUCU

1. Experience a Living Lab of the Future
Dive into one of the world’s most pressing issues:
population collapse. In Japan, where over half the
population will soon be over 65, innovation meets
urgency. Immerse yourself in a country at the forefront of
addressing societal challenges, exploring how technology,
culture, and policy converge to develop solutions the
world may depend on tomorrow.

2. Collaborate Globally, Innovate Locally
Begin with an online prep course in March, then join
intercultural teams at Nagoya University this May. Work
together to design and pitch a startup idea that
addresses the challenges of population collapse. Engage
in a Design Thinking Sprint to prototype solutions that
blend creativity and practicality, while gaining global
insights and connections.

3. Engage with Experts and Explore Bold Ideas
Participate in workshops, lectures, and field trips led by
industry leaders and academic pioneers. Explore thought-
provoking topics like AI & Ethics, AI & Epistemology, and
How to Know Truth, gaining new perspectives on
technology’s role in shaping the future of society.

4. Launch Your Future Career
Learn to think critically, adapt to change, and innovate
with purpose—essential skills for thriving in a dynamic
global environment. The program equips you with real-
world experience and tools to stand out as a future-ready
Leader.

5. Transformative Growth
From cultural immersion to high-impact networking,
NUCU offers a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity to grow
personally and professionally. Deepen your
understanding of global issues while forming lasting
connections with peers and mentors from around the
world.
